# Product

## Register

product

## Users

OpenList is used by people who manage remote drives, media files, uploads, shares, subscriptions, and storage settings from a browser. They are usually in an operational workflow: browsing files, moving or copying content, creating links, checking tasks, and correcting metadata or archive state.

## Product Purpose

OpenList provides a practical file-management surface over multiple cloud storage providers. Success means users can complete storage and media-management tasks quickly, with predictable controls, clear state, and recoverable management records.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, and direct. The interface should feel like a reliable admin tool: familiar controls, compact information, restrained visual treatment, and plain labels.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page composition, decorative card-heavy layouts, oversized hero typography, gratuitous animation, and unfamiliar custom controls where standard product UI patterns are expected.

## Design Principles

- Keep the current task visible and primary.
- Prefer dense, scannable controls over decorative explanation.
- Use existing Hope UI components and OpenList conventions before creating new patterns.
- Make destructive or duplicate actions explicit and confirmable.
- Preserve Chinese and English localization for user-facing labels.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use standard keyboard-accessible components from the existing UI library where possible. Maintain readable contrast in both light and dark modes, avoid motion that does not communicate state, and keep management tables usable at narrow widths.
